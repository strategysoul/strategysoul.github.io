Reducing plastics using behavioral nudges

My team and I dug into why India’s plastic ban flopped. Turns out, the average Indian uses 750 - 2143 plastic bags per year. Compare that to France, where it’s just 77. The numbers told us something simple: humans don’t handle bans well. Which raises the bigger question: are bans really the right way to change behavior, or are there smarter levers to pull?
